# 🇨🇱 Classic Chilean Sopaipillas
![Sopaipillas](recipes/sopaipilla.jpg)

This is the **traditional Chilean version made with pumpkin (zapallo)** and fried.  
Very common in **winter** and in **street food stands** across Chile.

---

## 🥖 Ingredients (about 12–15 sopaipillas)

- **250 g flour** (≈ 2 cups)
- **200 g pumpkin / zapallo** (cooked and mashed)
- **1 tsp salt**
- **1 tsp baking powder**
- **1 tbsp melted butter or lard**
- **Oil for frying**

**Optional (very Chilean touch)**  
- 1 pinch **ají de color** (paprika)

---

## 👨‍🍳 Preparation

### 1️⃣ Cook the pumpkin

1. Boil or steam the pumpkin until **soft**.
2. Drain well.
3. **Mash into a smooth purée**.

---

### 2️⃣ Make the dough

In a bowl mix:

- flour  
- baking powder  
- salt  

Add:

- pumpkin purée  
- melted butter  

Mix until you obtain a **soft but not sticky dough**.  
If needed, add **a little flour**.

---

### 3️⃣ Roll the dough

1. Roll the dough to about **5 mm thickness**.
2. Cut **circles (≈ 8–10 cm)** using a glass.

**Traditional step**

- Pierce each disk with a **fork** (prevents puffing while frying).

---

### 4️⃣ Fry

1. Heat oil to about **170–180 °C**.
2. Fry each sopaipilla **1–2 minutes per side** until golden.
3. Drain on **paper towels**.

---

## 🍽️ How Chileans eat them

### Savory

- With **pebre**  
  *(tomato, onion, cilantro, chili, vinegar)*
- With **mustard or ketchup**
- With **ají**

---

💡 **Tip:**  
Good sopaipillas should be **crispy outside**, **soft inside**, and slightly **orange** from the pumpkin.
